--- SECTION: THE CHALLENGE ---
section-title: The challenge
section-subtitle: Building thought leadership in a competitive space

section-body: The Consumer Insights campaign asked something specific of me as the sole creative on a cross-functional execution team: take a business objective (introducing a new framework called Return on Experience to the Canadian market) and translate it into a visual system that could hold together across six channels, two languages and full accessibility compliance, while continuously earning alignment from marketing peers, subject matter experts and a practice leader who held final approval. There was no creative team to delegate to. Direction, execution and quality control were all mine to own.

detail-brief: Position PwC Canada as a leading voice on consumer experience while breaking through in an increasingly saturated consulting landscape.
detail-timeline: Two and a half months from brief to delivery.
detail-constraints: Saturated thought leadership environment, newly simplified PwC global brand standard, bilingual and AODA-compliant delivery across all channels.

--- SECTION: STRATEGIC APPROACH ---
section-title: Strategic approach
section-subtitle: Unified and impactful creative delivery using brand toolkit

section-body: Every campaign requires finding a balance between capturing an audience by delivering a specific story and building brand equity that lasts. For the Consumer Insights campaign, I found that balance by working within PwC's globally defined brand toolkit—narrowing it down to build story-focused campaign creative that was distinctive without departing from brand.

subhead: The creative architecture
bullets:
- Colour palette: Applied a complementary colour family drawn from PwC's core palette across all deliverables, creating visual continuity while allowing flexibility across channels and formats.
- Photography with custom linework overlay: Selected Getty imagery capturing authentic moments of customer and employee experience, then applied custom overlays connecting human moments to data insights.
- Representational illustrations: Sourced base illustrations from Getty, then heavily adapted them to match campaign colour palette and visual style, making complex business frameworks feel approachable without losing rigour.
- Modular flexibility: Designed the system so components could be combined differently across channels without losing overall coherence.

--- SECTION: EXECUTION AND DELIVERY ---
section-title: Execution and delivery
section-subtitle: Consistent visuals across six touchpoints and dozens of deliverables

section-body: Keeping a visual identity coherent across six channels is a systems problem as much as a creative one. As the sole creative, I owned both ends: setting the visual framework and then producing every asset myself, from microsite layouts to programmatic banners to event materials, all while earning continuous alignment from channel specialists, subject matter experts and practice leaders who each had a stake in the outcome.

subhead: The deliverable ecosystem
section-body-2: Every deliverable in the ecosystem was produced in English and French and to full WCAG 2.0 accessibility standard; requirements were built-in from the first brief, not compliance checkboxes at the end.

bullets:
- Website: Developed visual approach, page layouts, data visualizations and image treatments for a multi-page microsite presenting Consumer Insights findings, working with the Digital Lead on implementation and accessibility.
- PDF report: Designed report structure and data visualizations, performed WCAG remediation and worked with production support to flow French translations into established templates.
- Social media: Created a suite of organic and paid assets for multiple releases; modular enough to create variety across placements while keeping the campaign visually coherent.
- Programmatic display: Designed four distinct content sets in standard IAB sizes with HTML5 animated versions, testing both photography and illustration-based approaches to optimize performance.

--- SECTION: IMPACT AND OUTCOMES ---
section-title: Impact and outcomes
section-subtitle: Delivering thought leadership that resonates

section-body: This campaign delivered on its core promise: Position PwC Canada as a credible, distinctive voice in a saturated thought leadership space without spending time or effort on campaign-specific creative infrastructure, while building broad equity for the core PwC brand.

subhead: What made this work
bullets:
- Strategic discipline: Choosing to build from the existing toolkit rather than invent something new was the right creative and commercial call. It delivered a stronger, more consistent campaign and built long-term brand equity simultaneously.
- Modular systems thinking: Designing for modularity from the start meant the visual system could adapt across channels, topics and formats without losing coherence—flexibility without fragmentation.
- Influence without authority: As the sole creative in a team of channel specialists and senior stakeholders, maintaining creative integrity required earning trust through clarity of thinking and consistency of output, not positional authority.
