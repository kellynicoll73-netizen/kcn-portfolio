--- SECTION: THE CHALLENGE ---
section-title: The challenge
section-subtitle: Accountability as a brand strategy

section-body: Some creative briefs ask you to make something beautiful. This one asked something harder: make something honest. The Trust Roadmap was PwC Canada's public commitment to measuring and reporting its own progress on ESG, DEI, Indigenous reconciliation and pay equity—the first initiative of its kind in Canada's Big 4. My role was to take that commitment and turn it into a creative system that could carry difficult truths and genuine progress with equal credibility, year after year, across every channel where stakeholders would encounter it.

detail-brief: Position PwC Canada as a leader in transparency by publicly reporting progress across ESG, DEI, Indigenous reconciliation, pay equity and operational accountability—creating market differentiation while supporting new consulting practices in trust and sustainability.
detail-timeline: FY22–FY24 (annual delivery cycle, March–June each year)
detail-constraints: First-of-its-kind initiative with no industry precedent; balancing transparency with organizational sensitivity; annual delivery cycle with compressed timelines; complex stakeholder approval processes across Editorial, Creative, Digital and practice leadership teams.

--- SECTION: STRATEGIC APPROACH ---
section-title: Strategic approach
section-subtitle: In-house ownership with a scalable creative system

section-body: The first Trust Roadmap had been outsourced and the result was a website that sat outside PwC's domain architecture, carried brand compliance risks and cost significantly more than our internal capabilities could deliver. Working with my Digital Lead and Studio leadership, I made the case to bring it in-house: more flexible, more brand-compliant, more accessible and substantially faster to produce—while keeping sensitive data and stakeholder conversations internal where they belonged. That proposal was approved and became the foundation for everything that followed.

subhead: The creative architecture
bullets:
- Colour palette: PwC core colours applied consistently across all deliverables, with accent colours used strategically to highlight progress metrics and key data points.
- Pixel-based visual system: Aligned with PwC's global 'The New Equation' positioning, adapted for a Canadian accountability context—building on global brand equity while preserving flexibility to tell local stories.
- Photography: Sourced imagery reflecting authentic moments of community partnership, workplace inclusion and environmental stewardship—warm, specific and human rather than generic corporate stock.
- Modular flexibility: The system worked across digital, social, print and video, maintaining a consistent brand experience while allowing content to flex year over year.

callout-sidebar-title: Process innovation for improved outcomes: Wireframe approvals
callout-sidebar-body: Previously, stakeholders would approve copy and then request structural changes once they saw content in final layouts, creating costly rework cycles across three teams. Introducing wireframe approvals moved structural and editorial decisions upstream, before any design work began. Stakeholders could see how content would sit in context and adjustments and edits early, allowing us to make design and production decisions based on final copy. By making this simple process swap, which reduced edit churn significantly, the entire FY24 project was completed in half the time required for FY22.

--- SECTION: EXECUTION AND DELIVERY ---
section-title: Execution and delivery
section-subtitle: Annual accountability across multiple channels

section-body: Delivering a multi-channel accountability campaign on a compressed annual timeline—with stakeholder approval chains running across Editorial, Creative, Digital and SMEs simultaneously—required the kind of creative leadership that designs the process as carefully as the product. Our eTeam methodology kept everyone moving in the same direction, a wireframe approach kept approval cycles from expanding and a modular creative system meant that year-over-year iteration was efficient rather than a rebuild from scratch. Efficiency, consistency and accountability.

detail-bilingual: All materials produced in English and French, as required by Quebec large-employer regulation and WCAG 2.0 Level AA compliant as required by Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act. These requirements were built in from the first brief, not retrofitted at the end.
detail-iterative: Each year incorporated lessons from the last—simplified creative approach informed by engagement data, UX improvements from CrazyEgg analysis and performance testing on programmatic ad formats, podcasts and paid social posts.

subhead: The deliverable ecosystem
bullets:
- Website: Three-page structure maintained consistently year over year—landing page, progress narrative, and accountability milestones timeline—with a separate accessible PDF for the detailed KPI table, enabling easy stakeholder distribution.
- External promotion: Paid and organic social media assets across multiple content releases; programmatic display advertising with both rich-media and animated approaches; blog and video content; press release coordination; Talking Trust podcast series; email campaigns with audience segmentation
- Internal education: toolkit including story and messaging toolkit, email signatures, digital signage and presentation graphics

--- SECTION: IMPACT AND OUTCOMES ---
section-title: Impact and outcomes
section-subtitle: First-of-its-kind accountability with measurable efficiency gains

section-body: Three years of consistent delivery made the case that transparency, done well, is a significant brand asset. The creative system that made it possible was still running strong in FY25, the wireframe approval methodology introduced for this project was adopted across other campaign work at the firm and demonstrated that corporate social responsibility—built into a creative system from the start—becomes a competitive advantage.

subhead: What made this work
bullets:
- Strategic in-sourcing: Recognizing that the vendor solution wasn't serving the business and proposing an internal alternative that delivered better brand compliance, better accessibility and substantially lower cost.
- Process innovation: Wireframe approvals transformed how Editorial, Creative and Digital teams collaborated—moving structural decisions upstream and compressing the FY24 delivery timeline to half that of FY22.
- Honest creative direction: Building a visual system capable of carrying difficult truths alongside genuine progress, without the campaign tipping into self-congratulation—consistency of tone was as important as consistency of design.
- Sustained year-over-year ownership: Same framework, same standards, iterative improvement—the kind of stewardship that turns a one-time initiative into an organizational capability.
