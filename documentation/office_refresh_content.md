--- SECTION: THE CHALLENGE ---
section-title: The challenge
section-subtitle: From extremes to excellence

section-body: Brand integration into physical space is a different kind of creative leadership—the work outlasts any campaign, and getting it wrong is expensive and permanent. As brand strategy and integration lead, I was the in-house brand authority on PwC Canada's most significant office transformation in over a decade: developing the "golden thread" design philosophy from scratch, directing every custom touchpoint from wayfinding to commissioned artwork, and advocating for accessibility and inclusion standards that ultimately set a new benchmark for the PwC global network.

detail-brief: PwC Canada envisioned workplace transformation across its national portfolio, beginning with three pilot locations—creating modern, flexible spaces that energized people while expressing brand identity in sophisticated, seamless ways.
detail-timeline: Initial project planning 2022; Vancouver completion summer 2023; Halifax completion late 2023; Toronto completion early 2024
detail-scope: Three pilot locations Vancouver, Halifax and Toronto—ranging from a single floor to a full two-floor flagship.
detail-constraints: Budget limitations, construction timelines, accessibility requirements, maintaining flexibility for future needs, expressing local community character while maintaining brand consistency.
detail-learning: Previous branded environments had swung between extremes—overbearing in the first wave, generic in the second. Neither felt authentically PwC. The refresh was the opportunity to get it right.
detail-agency: M Moser Associates led architecture, interior design and spatial planning across all three locations. My role was to lead client-side brand strategy and integration, ensuring the PwC brand was authentically and cohesively expressed throughout their design work, directing all custom branded touchpoints within the spaces they created.

--- SECTION: STRATEGIC APPROACH ---
section-title: Strategic approach
section-subtitle: The "golden thread" philosophy

section-body: The "golden thread" concept started with a simple premise: brand identity should be woven through a space, not applied to it. Rather than logos, message walls and overt colour, we asked what it would feel like to work in a space that was distinctly PwC without any single element announcing it. The answer was a layered system—spatial, material and visual—where every decision either references, supports or complements the brand.

subhead: Inspired by UK, refined for Canada
section-body-2: The PwC UK offices were energizing to visit. We took that ambition and applied it with more discipline: all colours drawn from PwC's core palette, all text in brand fonts, finishes consistent across locations.

subhead: Pilot through portfolio progression
bullets:
- Vancouver proved the concept on a single floor
- Halifax refined the wayfinding and accessibility approach across a full office
- Toronto became our flagship, the fullest expression of the philosophy, demonstrating how the framework could flex for different scales while maintaining brand consistency

section-body-3: Each installation informed the next, de-risking the portfolio-wide rollout while building stakeholder confidence.

subhead: The "golden thread" concept
section-body-4: The golden thread works through accumulation—no single element carries the brand alone, but together they create something unmistakably PwC:
bullets:
- Layered lighting creating atmosphere and wayfinding
- Regional colour palettes each anchored to a PwC core colour, with all finishes and furnishings referencing or complementing
- Textural storytelling through FSC-certified woods and materials
- Biophilic cues connecting occupants to place
- Geometric patterns derived from brand architecture, appearing in acoustic treatments, millwork and wall treatments
- Curated photography showcasing local communities and natural environments

section-body-5: The brand emerges through experience—felt, not just seen. Distinctively branded, but more like a constant murmur and not a deafening shout.

--- SECTION: VISUAL LANGUAGE SYSTEM DEVELOPMENT ---
section-title: Visual language system development
section-subtitle: The golden thread tying everything together

section-body: A brand philosophy only works if it translates into specific, repeatable decisions. In collaboration with M Moser, I developed the visual elements and touchpoints that brought the 'golden thread' to life.

detail-iconography: Based on PwC standards, adapted for wayfinding and signage across all locations.
detail-photography: Selected and approved images showcasing local character while maintaining visual and brand consistency, celebrating the communities where we work while delivering striking focal points.

subhead: Custom pieces
bullets:
- Illustrated artworks expressing PwC global values of Care, Work Together, Integrity, Reimagine the Possible and Make a Difference
- Custom pattern for modular room divider system
- Commissioned artwork installations for kitchen, cafe and reception spaces
- Geometric acoustic treatments doubling as brand expression

callout-sidebar-title: Accessibility, inclusion and cultural leadership

callout-sidebar-subhead-1: Best-in-class accessibility
callout-sidebar-body-1: Working with the Rick Hansen Foundation, we achieved certification through comprehensive accessibility integration:
bullets-accessibility:
- All wayfinding in braille and raised text where accessible to touch
- AODA-compliant sizing and preferred upper/lower case across all text applications
- Wheelchair-accessible heights for wayfinding and labelling signage
- Custom visual-contrast treatments for barrier-free circulation
- A variety of working spaces for varying organizational, cognitive and neurodiverse working styles

callout-sidebar-subhead-2: First in network inclusion advocacy
callout-sidebar-body-2: Working with PwC I&D leadership, Operations and an external gender consultant, we delivered the first gender-neutral washrooms in the PwC global network.

callout-sidebar-subhead-3: Indigenous artists program (Toronto)
callout-sidebar-body-3: Collaborated with Operations and the Indigenous Inclusion Circle to commission two female Indigenous Ontario artists whose works are prominently displayed in client-facing spaces with signage I designed highlighting the artists and their pieces.

callout-sidebar-result: Spaces that work for, and welcome, as many as possible; honoring accessibility, inclusion and cultural respect as strategic priorities, not afterthoughts.
